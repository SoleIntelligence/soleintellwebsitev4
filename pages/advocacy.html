Legislative Advocacy

Advocating for Podiatric Medicine on the Hill

Dr. Sorensen has traveled to Washington, D.C. to meet with congressional offices and advocate for legislation affecting podiatric physicians and their patients. His MHA background informs a policy-focused approach to the issues that matter most at the federal level.

With U.S. Senator Todd Young (R-IN) and Dr. Patrick DeHeer, DPM, 100th President of the American Podiatric Medical Association. Dr. Sorensen participated in the swearing-in ceremony. · Washington, D.C., November 2025
In a congressional office discussing the long-term effects of Medicare physician fee schedule cuts on patient access, alongside APMA 99th President Dr. Brooke Bisbee, DPM · Capitol Hill, November 2025

Photos: Lisa Helfert Photography · November 2025

Medicare Physician Fee Schedule Reform

Advocated directly with congressional offices on the long-term, compounding effects of recent Medicare fee schedule updates on physician access and practice viability — particularly for surgical specialists in rural and underserved markets.

The Diabetic Shoe Bill

Represented podiatric medicine in legislative discussions around therapeutic footwear coverage for diabetic patients — a critical preventive measure for reducing amputation rates and long-term healthcare costs.

Speaking

Available to Speak

Dr. Sorensen has spoken at multiple residency programs on integrating AI into research and clinical workflows. Topics below are available for grand rounds, residency programs, conferences, and institutional events.

01

Current AI Tool Usage — What Actually Works

A practical tour of the AI tools worth adding to your clinical and research workflow, and the ones worth skipping.

02

AI in Healthcare & Podiatry — The Landscape

Where medicine and AI intersect today, where it's going, and what it means for podiatric practice and reimbursement.

03

LLMs in Daily Life, Clinic & Surgery

How to incorporate large language models into documentation, patient education, and surgical planning without compromising care.

04

Using AI for Biomedical Research

Practical workflows for literature review, poster development, manuscript prep, and study design using AI tools.

05

What Tools Matter — and Which Don't?

Cutting through the hype. A frank conversation about signal vs. noise in the healthcare AI space, grounded in real clinical use.

The right tools don't just save time — they create space. More time for complex cases, for research, for teaching. And more time for everything outside the clinic that keeps you sharp.

— Peter Sorensen, DPM, MHA
